RFID ATTENDANCE REGISTER SYSTEM

An RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) attendance system uses RFID tags and readers to track attendance for students, faculty, or staff.
Here are some things to know about RFID attendance systems:

How it works

Students use an RFID tag, which can be a card, smartwatch, or part of their uniform, to mark their attendance by placing it near an RFID reader.
The reader triggers an electromagnetic pulse that transmits the student's digital data to the system. Benefits

RFID attendance systems can

Automate attendance: Reduce the need for manual attendance taking and paperwork
Improve accuracy: Ensure that students are marked in on time and don't leave early
Improve security: RFID cards are difficult to tamper with, and data is encrypted and locked
Improve efficiency: Save time and resources, and free up teachers to spend more time teaching
Improve communication: Parents and students can receive notifications about attendance, and parents can apply for leave online

Types of RFID tags

There are active, passive, and semi-passive RFID tags. Active tags have their own power source, while passive tags receive power from the reader.
